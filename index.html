Learn More About Oral Health

   

Cavities (also called tooth decay) are damaged areas in your teeth that turn into small holes over time. They happen when bacteria in your mouth feed on sugars from the food and drinks you consume. These bacteria create acids that slowly wear away the outer layer of your tooth, called enamel. If this damage continues, it can go deeper into the tooth and cause pain, sensitivity, or even infection. Cavities can start small and may not hurt at first, which is why regular dental checkups are important.

Cavities are one of the most common health conditions, but they are also one of the most preventable.

How do we get cavities?

  • Eating sugary or sticky foods (like candy, soda, or snacks)
  • Not brushing and flossing regularly
  • Bacteria building up in plaque on your teeth
  • Frequent snacking or sipping drinks throughout the day
  • Not visiting the dentist for cleanings and checkups

Over time, these habits allow acid to damage your teeth, leading to cavities. The good news is cavities are preventable with good oral hygiene and regular dental care.

What is Gingivitis?

Gingivitis is the early stage of gum disease, and it’s very common, but also reversible. It happens when plaque, a sticky film of bacteria, builds up along the gumline and irritates your gums.

When plaque is not removed with brushing and flossing, it produces toxins that cause your gums to become red, swollen, and more sensitive. You may notice bleeding when brushing or flossing, which is one of the first warning signs.

The good news is that gingivitis does not cause permanent damage at this stage. With good oral hygiene and regular dental cleanings, your gums can return to a healthy state.

Common Signs of Gingivitis:

  • Red or swollen gums
  • Bleeding when brushing or flossing
  • Tender or sensitive gums
  • Bad breath

If gingivitis is not treated, it can progress into a more serious oral condition called periodontitis.
